Caramel Brownies

This recipe is the kind of indulgence that instantly feels like a treat worth savoring—rich, decadent, and layered with irresistible flavor in every bite. Caramel brownies bring together the deep, fudgy intensity of classic chocolate brownies with the smooth, buttery sweetness of caramel, creating a dessert that’s both bold and beautifully balanced. The brownie base is dense and moist, with a soft, melt-in-your-mouth texture and a rich cocoa flavor that lingers just long enough before giving way to ribbons of golden caramel woven throughout. As the brownies bake, the caramel softens and settles into pockets and swirls, forming glossy, slightly gooey layers that contrast perfectly with the tender crumb of the chocolate. Each bite offers a little something different—sometimes more chocolate-forward, sometimes rich with that silky caramel pull—making the experience feel dynamic and satisfying from start to finish. The edges develop a slight chew while the center stays irresistibly soft, giving you that perfect bakery-style texture that so many people love. What makes these caramel brownies especially memorable is how the sweetness never feels overwhelming. The slight bitterness of the cocoa balances the caramel’s buttery richness, while a hint of salt (if added) can elevate the flavors even further, creating that crave-worthy sweet-and-salty combination. Whether enjoyed warm with the caramel still soft and flowing or cooled to let the flavors settle into a more structured bite, these brownies offer a comforting, luxurious experience that feels both homemade and elevated. Paired with a glass of cold milk, a scoop of vanilla ice cream, or simply enjoyed on their own, caramel brownies are the kind of dessert that invites you to slow down and savor each bite. They’re perfect for sharing at gatherings, gifting to friends, or keeping all to yourself for those moments when only something deeply chocolatey and unapologetically indulgent will do.

Ingredients

3 Eggs 2 Cups of Chocolate Chips 1/2 cup of Butter 1 teaspoon Baking powder 1 teaspoon Vanilla extract 1 cup of brown sugar 1/2 cup of Flour 1/4 of a cup of Cocoa powder 2 cups of Chocolate chips 2 tablespoons of Maple Syrup 14 oz of sweetened condensed milk

Recipe

  • Melt butter in a saucepan with golden syrup or maple syrup and salt
  • Add sweetened condensed milk and mix until well-combined
    		•
  • Then cook, stirring very frequently, over low heat until it changes from white to a caramel colour. You can get away with only stirring occasionally for the first few minutes, but as you start to see steam, be sure to stir constantly – albeit leisurely – to ensure it doesn’t catch on the base. If the caramel happens to catch on the base, you will end up with tiny little brown bits in the caramel. It might look like a big deal when it’s in the saucepan but don’t worry – once it’s baked up inside the brownie and you bite into it, you won’t even notice.
    		•
  • Take it off the stove, then keep stirring for another 30 seconds just to cool it down a touch otherwise the residual heat in the saucepan might still make the caramel catch on the base. Cover, then keep warm while you make the batter. It needs to be pourable when you use it.
    		•
  • Firstly, make the Brownie batter. It’s very easy and all happens in one bowl. If you want visuals of the steps, pop over to my brownie recipe; Set aside a heaped ½-cup of the batter for the topping. Then pour the remainder into a 20cm/8″ square pan. A lamington pan will also work well, the slice is just a bit thinner than pictured;
  • Pour the caramel over as evenly as you can. Note: If your caramel has cooled down to the point that it’s not pourable, just reheat it on a medium stove until it’s pourable again (this literally takes 1 minute). You can’t really spread the caramel because it sinks into the batter once poured. So where you pour is what you get!
  • Dollop the reserved ½-cup of brownie batter across the top; Then swirl a butter knife across the surface a few times in large sweeping motions. This is not so much about creating swirl effects on the surface because they don’t really stay once baked, as you can see in Step 6. It’s more about sort-of spreading the brownie batter across the caramel; and
    		•
  • Bake – 30 minutes for very-messy-hands-gooey, as pictured, or 35 minutes for sensible gooey.
